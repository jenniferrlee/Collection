    The book Supplemento al dizionario italiano by Bruno Munari is a book in which he explains the meaning of the characteristic and deeply codified Italian hand gestures, many of which originated in Napoli before spreading to the rest of Italy and in some cases the rest of the world. The book is about the size of my hand, from the bottom of the palm to the tip of my middle finger in length. It is bound by PUR perfect binding method, making it easy to open the book in 180 degrees. In each body spread, the left page has the meanings explained in 4 different languages - Italian, French, English and German. The right page has a black and white image of the hand gesture that is being explained.
